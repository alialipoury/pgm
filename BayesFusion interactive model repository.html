Description

Hepar II is a Bayesian network model for the diagnosis of liver disorders, generously contributed to the community by Agnieszka Onisko. The orange colored nodes represent diseases, the blue nodes represent patient history, including risk factors, and green nodes represent observations and test results.

Reference

The primary reference for the Hepar II model is Agnieszka Onisko's doctoral dissertation (available at: http://aragorn.pb.bialystok.pl/~aonisko): Agnieszka Onisko. Probabilistic Causal Models in Medicine: Application to Diagnosis of Liver Disorders. Ph.D. Dissertation, Institute of Biocybernetics and Biomedical Engineering, Polish Academy of Science, Warsaw, March 2003.

BayesFusion interactive model repository

You have entered BayesFusion's interactive model repository. Please note that we are recording user actions but are doing this for the sole purpose of technical improvements to the repository. Should you not wish this, please exit the repository.

This website is powered by BayesBox - our software for sharing and exploring Bayesian networks and other types of probabilistic graphical models. If you are interested in having such a repository self-hosted or managed for you by BayesFusion, or would like to integrate this technology into your software, please contact us.

You can examine interactively models included in the repository by first selecting them from the pop-up menu in the upper-left corner. Models are divided into several classes according to their types and sizes. Once you have selected a model, you can enter and clear evidence (or decisions, in case of influence diagrams) and observe the results of interest (the models are updated automatically). You can download any model to your local storage by pressing on the Download button.

You can also view the results of computation (i.e., posterior marginals or the expected utilities) for selected nodes. To view the results, hover over the check-mark on the node or operate on the list of nodes on the right-hand side by expanding and contracting individual nodes. To enter evidence for a variable or to set a decision, click on the node representing this variable and select an outcome. Alternatively, double-click on the bar charts on the right-hand side, similarly to what you would do in GeNIe. To clear an observation, double click on the observed state in the bar chart or click on the node and select Clear evidence (or Retract decision, in case of influence diagrams).
